1. Real-Time Waste Detection Using YOLO, SSD, and Faster R-CNN Integrated with CNN-Based Classification
Abstract Summary: This study addresses the inefficiencies of manual waste sorting by proposing a real-time automated classification system. It integrates object detection models with a MobileNetV2 CNN classifier. YOLOv11 proved to be the superior model, balancing rapid processing speed with high precision.
Main Objective / Goal: To systematically compare YOLO, SSD, and Faster R-CNN architectures to identify the most accurate and scalable model for real-time waste classification.
Methodology / Approach Used: 
Algorithms / Models: YOLOv11, SSD-MobileNetV3, Faster R-CNN (ResNet50 FPN), MobileNetV2 (CNN classifier).
System design: Transfer learning on TACO and Domestic Waste datasets; an end-to-end pipeline detecting objects first, then classifying materials.
Key Results / Outcomes: YOLOv11 achieved the highest mAP@0.5:0.95 (55.69%) and the fastest inference (71.1 FPS). The MobileNetV2 classifier reached 85.02% accuracy.
Limitations / Drawbacks: 
Technical: Accuracy is constrained by the limited diversity and scale of the training datasets.
Practical: Lacks hardware integration (e.g., robotic arms) to translate vision outputs into mechanical sorting.
Research Gap Identified: Previous literature lacked side-by-side evaluations of these specific models under strict real-time performance criteria.
Improvement Suggestions: Replace the two-step detection/classification pipeline with a unified YOLOv11-seg (Instance Segmentation) model. Generate Synthetic Data (GANs) to simulate diverse lighting and overlapping waste scenarios, expanding the training set without manual labor.
2. National Circular Economy Roadmap for Plastics in India
Abstract Summary: This roadmap synthesizes research across policy, business models, and recycling technologies to address India's plastic waste crisis. It highlights the vast, undocumented informal sector and a critical lack of systematic material recovery facilities. It provides actionable recommendations for transitioning to circular business models.
Main Objective / Goal: To identify barriers to the circular economy for plastics in India and propose strategies to improve data transparency, infrastructure, and socio-economic integration.
Methodology / Approach Used: 
Algorithms / Models: Qualitative thematic analysis (Dedoose software).
System design: Semi-structured interviews, extensive literature reviews, and case study evaluations of 16 local initiatives.
Key Results / Outcomes: Identified missing technology, inadequate collection of flexible packaging, and the critical need for public-private incubators and financial incentives to scale community efforts.
Limitations / Drawbacks: 
Technical: Relies heavily on intermittent expert estimates; lacks real-time, quantitative data tracking.
Practical: The dominance of informal, unregulated labor creates severe roadblocks for standardized policy implementation.
Research Gap Identified: An absence of harmonized data collection methodologies and a disconnect between formal government policies and the informal repair/collection economy.
Improvement Suggestions: Implement LLM-RAG pipelines accessible via mobile devices to educate informal workers on local SWM rules in regional languages. Utilize Software-only alternatives (like crowdsourced mobile apps) to track waste quantities quantitatively without relying on physical infrastructure.
3. A Graph-Retrieval-Augmented Generation Framework Enhances Decision-Making in the Circular Economy (CircuGraphRAG)
Abstract Summary: Standard large language models frequently hallucinate industrial codes when queried about circular economy regulations. This paper introduces CircuGraphRAG, which grounds LLMs in a domain-specific knowledge graph. The framework dramatically improves factual accuracy for waste-to-resource synergies.
Main Objective / Goal: To mitigate LLM hallucinations regarding industrial waste classifications and emissions, enabling reliable, regulatory-ready decision support.
Methodology / Approach Used: 
Algorithms / Models: CircuGraphRAG, LLaMA3, Qwen, DeepSeek, FAISS vector indexing.
System design: Translates natural language into SPARQL to query a structured knowledge graph (linking EWC, NACE, ISIC codes), combined with dense embeddings.
Key Results / Outcomes: Achieved perfect ROUGE-L F1 scores (1.0) on complex multi-hop queries, compared to baselines scoring <0.08, while halving response time.
Limitations / Drawbacks: 
Technical: LLMs show instability in template matching; removing structural templates drops accuracy to zero.
Practical: The static knowledge graph requires continuous, manual updating to reflect new real-world regulations.
Research Gap Identified: The direct application of KGs and GraphRAG within the circular economy was virtually non-existent, causing generic LLMs to fail at multi-hop regulatory reasoning.
Improvement Suggestions: Employ Multi-Agent Systems where one agent actively monitors the web for policy updates to autonomously maintain the Knowledge Graph.

Main Objective / Goal: To evaluate the true impact of IoT-based vehicle routing optimization and determine if dataset types (simulated vs. real) skew reported performance.
Methodology / Approach Used: 
Algorithms / Models: Meta-analysis (Mann-Whitney U test, Forest plots) of heuristic and mathematical algorithms.
System design: Systematic literature screening comparing IoT vs. non-IoT routing scenarios.
Key Results / Outcomes: Collection distance was reduced by an average of 21.51%. However, simulated datasets reported a 39.79% reduction, while real-world data showed only a 12.37% reduction.
Limitations / Drawbacks: 
Technical: Most primary studies failed to report individual variances or standard errors.

Research Gap Identified: The significant "reality gap" between idealized algorithmic simulations and chaotic, real-world urban logistics.
Improvement Suggestions: Integrate Predictive Analytics (XGBoost) using live traffic APIs rather than static IoT fill-levels. Use Software-only driver apps to bypass physical sensor failures that disrupt routing.

Overall Insights from 23 Papers
Common Trends:
The Shift to Advanced Edge Vision: There is a ubiquitous transition from slow, two-stage object detectors (like Faster R-CNN) to highly efficient, real-time single-stage models, with YOLOv11 and Instance Segmentation emerging as the dominant standard for complex, cluttered environments (e.g., mixed waste, e-waste, agriculture).
Grounding AI with RAG & Knowledge Graphs: A major trend in high-stakes domains (public health, nuclear governance, circular economy) is the movement away from unconstrained LLMs toward Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) and Graph-RAG. These architectures are proven to drastically reduce hallucinations and semantic drift by anchoring outputs in verified, multi-source external documents.
Mathematical & Predictive Routing: Logistics and urban planning are moving from static, point-based methods to continuous, dynamic probability models (like Network KDE and XGBoost). XGBoost and LSTM repeatedly demonstrate superior capability in forecasting complex, non-linear environmental and socio-economic generation patterns.
Common Gaps:
The IoT Hardware Bottleneck: A glaring gap across multiple studies is the massive capital expenditure and environmental fragility of physical IoT hardware (e.g., ultrasonic sensors, reward bins). These systems suffer heavily from water damage, vandalism, and network limitations (NB-IoT), causing a severe "reality gap" where simulated routing outperforms real-world deployments.
Lack of Real-Time Educational Feedback: Many detection systems passively monitor anomalies (like factory errors or contaminated bins) but lack conversational, human-in-the-loop interfaces to actually educate the user or correct the behavior in real time.
Bounding Box Limitations in Cluttered Environments: Studies relying on bounding boxes (YOLOv5/v8) consistently struggle with overlapping, clustered, or camouflaged objects, highlighting the need for pixel-level instance segmentation.
Opportunities for Innovation:
The "Software-as-a-Sensor" Paradigm: By replacing expensive physical IoT hardware with crowdsourced mobile applications, projects can achieve zero-CapEx scalability. Using citizen smartphone cameras powered by YOLOv11-seg entirely bypasses the vulnerabilities of physical smart bins.
Multi-Agent Synergies: Combining predictive analytics (XGBoost/LSTM) with Multi-Agent RAG systems offers immense potential. For example, one agent can predict waste generation hotspots, while another drafts legally compliant municipal routing orders based on those predictions.
Synthetic Data Generation (GANs): Across computer vision and remote sensing, models are bottlenecked by small or unvaried datasets. Utilizing GANs to simulate severe weather, occlusions, and cloud cover provides a massive opportunity to build bulletproof algorithms prior to real-world deployment.

Overall Insights from 23 Papers
Common trends:
Shift to Edge AI and Real-Time Vision: The industry relies heavily on the YOLO family (especially YOLOv11) due to its unmatched balance of inference speed and accuracy for complex object detection (e-waste, school waste, medical diagnostics).
RAG as a Governance Standard: Retrieval-Augmented Generation is now the default method for mitigating LLM hallucinations in high-stakes fields (medicine, climate policy, enterprise governance).
Hybrid Graph Architectures: Traditional RAG is rapidly evolving into GraphRAG and Hyper-RAG to map complex, high-order regulatory and industrial relationships.
Common gaps:
The "Simulation vs. Reality" Gap: Vision models and IoT routing algorithms perform exceptionally well on clean datasets and simulations but degrade severely under real-world clutter, overlapping objects, and variable traffic conditions.
Data Scarcity & Hardware Costs: A severe lack of multimodal datasets (fusing vision, depth, and text) persists. Furthermore, reliance on heavy IoT sensor infrastructure (like physical bin sensors) poses massive scaling and maintenance burdens in developing regions.
Latency and Computational Overhead: Multi-stage reasoning (like MEGA-RAG) and massive models (70B parameters) introduce latency and high costs that prohibit deployment on edge devices.
Opportunities for innovation:
Software-Only Architectures: Removing fragile IoT hardware entirely and replacing it with predictive analytics (LSTM/XGBoost) and citizen mobile-app inputs to map risk and route vehicles.
Instance Segmentation: Upgrading basic object detection to YOLOv11 instance segmentation to perfectly identify heavily deformed, overlapping, or translucent waste.
Multi-Agent RAG Systems: Deploying multi-agent architectures to cross-reference visual detections with local laws or medical policies dynamically.
Synthetic Data Integration: Using GANs to artificially generate massive datasets of extreme real-world clutter to train more robust models without manual labeling.
